Paris, ah, I envy you.

If you're comfortable with makefiles, compilers, and all that,  
probably the best way would be to compile wxPython into a different  
folder. I've always been pissed off by the fact that Apple never felt  
it appropriate to put in a /usr/local structure, so I'd probably put  
in /usr/local/lib and link against it from there.

Once you have something working, _please_ distributes as a full,  
complete, statically linked binary. Memory is cheap, and there's  
really no reason why people should jump through the hoops Apple's non- 
standard directory structure imposes. (Actually, I think most things  
should be distributed in this form anyway.)

Amsterdam, Bruges, Ypres, and Paris a couple of months ago. Except  
for the high cost of housing, man, I wish I lived in Europe. (Paris  
especially). So much to see, so much to do.
